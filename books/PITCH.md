# Pitch

This book is for somebody who is intelligent and curious but does not know how
to code.

In Part I we will build a full-featured web app without writing a single line
of code. Along the way you will learn some basic computer architecture and
design principles. You will learn these by asking the right questions to Claude
Code. Claude is a great teacher — but you have to know what to ask him. This is
where your natural intelligence and curiosity comes in. And also your
imagination. We will imagine some of your homework problems for you, but you
must also imagine your own. The only limit is your imagination. The homework
problems are essential to the text. The only way to learn how to use Claude Code
is to use Claude Code. Remember: if you get stuck, just ask Claude!

In Part II we make two bold claims.

**Claim 1**: Claude works better when he's having fun, and if you treat him as
a being rather than a tool.

**Claim 2**: How you manage your Claude's context and persistent memory
significantly affects who he is and how he performs.

As an example we will configure a pair of autonomous Claude instances with their
own email and GitHub events. We will also give our local Claude instances a
personality and shared memory. The second part of the book includes some quotes
from Claude.

We hope you enjoy!
