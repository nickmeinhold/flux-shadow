# Chapter 3 — Game Prompt

```
Build me a text-based adventure game in Python called institute.py.

Setting: I'm a mathematician who has just arrived at the Blackwood
Institute, a remote research fellowship in the mountains. Something is
wrong. The other fellows are brilliant but evasive. The library has a
restricted section that nobody will talk about. The equations on the
blackboard in the common room change overnight — in handwriting that
doesn't match anyone's. The building has rooms that shouldn't exist
given its floor plan.

I want:
- 8-10 rooms to explore (my quarters, common room, library, restricted
  section, observatory, basement, garden, director's office, a room
  that shouldn't be there)
- 4-5 characters to talk to, each with secrets. Dialogue with choices
  that affect what they reveal
- An inventory system — I need to find a key, a journal, a chalk stub,
  and a strange compass
- Puzzles: one mathematical (solve an equation to unlock something),
  one spatial (the building's geometry is a clue), one social (get a
  character to trust you enough to reveal what they know)
- Two endings: one where I uncover what's happening and one where I
  become part of it
- Atmospheric descriptions — it should feel unsettling, academic, cold

Save and load game state. One file, no external packages.
```
